Submit your plea online

For some offences you may submit your plea online. In most cases that means you wonâ€™t need to come to court.

  • You must be pleading guilty to ALL charges
  • Serious charges are not included
  • The case must be listed to be heard at the Neighbourhood Justice Centre

You should seek legal advice to help you decide

What you will need

When filling out this form, you will need the complaint and summons or notice to appear. This explains the details of the offence and your court hearing information.

You must submit your online guilty plea at least 2 business days before your court date.

What happens in court

Your plea of guilty will be provided to the court. The magistrate will either:

  • deal with the charge without you being there, or
  • require that you come to court and adjourn the matter to another date.

After the hearing

If your online guilty plea is accepted by the court, we will let you know in writing of any penalty imposed. This may take up to 7 days after the hearing date.

If your online guilty plea is not accepted by the court, you will be advised of the new hearing details. There may be consequences if you fail to appear then - for example, the magistrate may issue a warrant for your arrest.
